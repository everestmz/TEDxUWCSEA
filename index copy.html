About

What is TEDx?

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TED has created a program called TEDx. TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. Our event is called TEDxUWCSEA, where x = independently organized TED event. At our TEDxUWCSEA event, TEDTalks video and live speakers will combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events, including ours, are self-organized.

Speakers

Speakers at TEDx UWCSEA

Durreen Shahnaz

Durreen is the Founder and Managing Director of Impact Investment Shujog and the Founder and Chairwoman of Impact Investment Exchange Asia. With the vision to impact one billion lives through democratizing social capital markets, Durreen has built a track record as a successful academic, banker, media executive and social entrepreneur. Speaker Page

Dr. Ray Dunn

Dr. Ray Dunn has studied cell biology at institutes such as Vanderbilt University and Harvard University. He will use his background to give us an insight into the beauty of the embryo and its contributions to modern science. Speaker Page

Ned Phillips

Ned, age 47 who is originally from Scotland has been living in Asia since 1991. Having traveled extensively through Asia he has settled and lived in Singapore for the past 11 years, and previously lived in Hong Kong for 12 years. Ned has a wife and two kids and currently works in the finance industry. Speaker Page

Samarth Chawla

My name is Samarth Chawla. I am a 17-year-old Indian living in Singapore. My Ted talk is on “rethinking consumerism”. It is inspired by my love for economics. In the talk, I discuss how and why we need to change what it means to be a consumer in the 21st century. Speaker Page

Abhisvara Sinha

Abhisvara is a 17-year old Indian girl studying at UWCSEA, Singapore. An avid writer, history buff, and eternal traveller, Abhisvara’s interests are the inspiration for her TEDtalk in which she assesses the presence of walls, literal and figurative, as divisive forces. Speaker Page

Aaron Maniam

Having graduated at top of his class at Yale and Oxford. Aaron now works in the Singapore Government with the Ministry of Trade and Industry after working as a diplomat in Washington and as political strategist at various think tanks. Speaker Page

David Munro

David moved to Asia 21 years ago to run the Asian leg of a global derivatives book for UBS and was subsequently SVP and Head of Trading for Sanwa/UFJ. After 10 years of Asian banking, he founded a long/short volatility hedge fund and then joined a larger fund that won the “Best Asian-Based Macro Fund” award four years in a row. Speaker Page

Johnson Chen

Mr. Chen works in the research industry as the Managing Partner of Clearbridge Accelerator and Clearbridge BioMedics. With co-funding support from Singapore’s National Research Foundation, he works to nurture and develop high-technology start-ups. Speaker Page

Naila Segule

Parented by Tanzanians, born and raised in the United States, and participation in multiple diversity clubs including attendance of several diversity conferences have led Naila to explore the many factors that shape one’s identity. Speaker Page

Akshay Smit

Akshay is a senior year student at UWCSEA with a deep fascination for maths and philosophy. His exploration of the combination of the two is the basis of his talk 'Limitations of Logic - or the Human Brain?' Speaker Page

Mahboob Mahmood

Mahboob received his J.D. from Columbia Law School in 1986, his M.A. in Political Science from The Johns Hopkins University in 1987 and B.A. in Politics from Princeton University in 1981. He is the Founder and CEO of Knowledge Platform. Speaker Page

Ayesha Khanna

Ayesha has a BA (honors) in Economics from Harvard University, an MS in Operations Research from Columbia University and is completing her PhD on urban information infrastructures at the London School of Economics. Speaker Page
